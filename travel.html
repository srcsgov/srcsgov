Immigration, Travel, and Rules

General Immigration Policies

The Sovereign Maritime Commonwealth of Fairchuck welcomes visitors and immigrants under the following regulations:

  • All visitors must arrive via connecting flights through Guam to Chuuk International Airport (TKK).
  • Visa application fees are USD $500, non-refundable.
  • Visas are granted for a maximum stay of 30 days, with extensions subject to approval.
  • Proof of onward travel and sufficient financial resources are required for all applicants.
Citizenship Privileges

Citizens of Fairchuck have unique privileges:

  • Unrestricted travel to allied nations even during wartime.
  • Priority access to emergency evacuation services.
  • Eligibility for maritime resource subsidies and government benefits.
Travel During Wartime

Special regulations for travel during wartime include:

  • Only citizens and approved diplomats may enter or exit the territory.
  • All travelers must carry an official wartime travel permit issued by the Fairchuck Ministry of Defense.
  • Commercial flights may be suspended; alternative arrangements will be made through the national maritime fleet.
Prohibited Items and Activities

The following are prohibited when entering or staying in Fairchuck:

  • Firearms and other dangerous weapons without government authorization.
  • Unauthorized collection of marine life or cultural artifacts.
  • Activities that threaten local biodiversity or violate traditional customs.
Visa Requirements and Costs
  • Business Visa: $1,500; requires proof of company registration and financial records.
  • Employment Visa: $2,000; requires an approved work contract and government verification.
  • Family Visit Visa: $1,200; requires proof of relationship and host guarantee.
  • Official Visa: $2,500; restricted to government and diplomatic missions.
  • Other Visa: $3,000; must provide strong justification for entry.
  • Student Visa: $1,800; requires university admission confirmation and financial support proof.
  • Tourist Visa: $1,000; must book government-approved accommodations and tours.
  • Transit Visa: $700; valid for 48 hours with proof of onward travel.
Counterintelligence Office Authority

The Counterintelligence Office of Fairchuuk (CIOF) holds the authority to conduct thorough security checks and verifications on all individuals entering, residing in, or departing from the country. The following are the key powers and enforcement rights of the CIOF:

  • Background Verification: Conducts full checks on criminal records, political affiliations, and previous international activities.
  • Financial Scrutiny: May investigate bank statements, funding sources, and unexplained financial transactions.
  • Communication Monitoring: Reserves the right to monitor phone calls, emails, and internet usage under national security directives.
  • Employment & Business Audits: Can inspect workplaces, request tax compliance reports, and verify job contracts.
  • Border Screening: Conducts biometric scans, luggage inspections, and random secondary questioning at all entry and exit points.
  • Residency Spot Checks: May conduct unannounced visits to verify legal stay and intended activities.
  • Association Tracking: Monitors affiliations with foreign groups, organizations, and suspected dissidents.
  • Visa Compliance Investigations: Can revoke or deny visas if any discrepancies or security concerns arise.
  • Deportation & Detention: Holds the authority to detain or expel individuals deemed a threat to national security.
  • Surveillance Authorization: Reserves the right to place individuals under discreet observation without prior notice.

Failure to comply with CIOF regulations or refusal to cooperate with security procedures may result in denial of entry, visa cancellation, or legal action under the Fairchuuk Security Act.
